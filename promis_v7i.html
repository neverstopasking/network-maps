I'm curious about a triple agent named Robert Maxwell who sold stolen software to the KGB, to Guatemala, to our nuclear labs, and to intelligence agencies around the world who had no idea that their agencies could be spied on.
And the people who figured it out
kept ending up dead.
The CIA and Mossad have had a formal intelligence-sharing partnership since nineteen fifty-one, which is just a fun fact and is completely unrelated to this story
about a husband and wife named Bill and Nancy Hamilton who in nineteen eighty-two built a piece of software called PROMIS for the Department of Justice. It was revolutionary.
PROMIS could connect law-enforcement databases that had never been able to talk to each other, linking cases, warrants, suspects, and financial records across jurisdictions.
The DOJ took it, refused to pay for it, and drove the company into bankruptcy.
A federal judge later ruled the government had taken the software through trickery, fraud, and deceit — his words.
Meanwhile a private security firm, Wackenhut Corporation, had been running weapons manufacturing at the Cabazon Indian Reservation. Sovereign land. Outside normal U.S. jurisdiction.
Wackenhut happened to be staffed with former intelligence officers and was once represented by future CIA director William Casey — which I'm sure is also completely unrelated.
In July nineteen eighty-one tribal vice chairman Fred Alvarez and two others started asking questions about the operation. All three were later found shot execution-style. The murders were never solved.
Wackenhut is now GEO Group, one of the largest for-profit prison companies in America, whose first contract was with the Department of Justice and which just posted record profits on government immigration detention contracts. Just another fun fact.
Two years later a man named Michael Riconosciuto testified that he had installed a back door into PROMIS that allowed whoever controlled it to spy on the system using it. That work was done on the Cabazon reservation where Wackenhut operated.
Later, Robert Maxwell distributed PROMIS worldwide —
and Senator John Tower was allegedly paid to help walk the software into Sandia and Los Alamos.
America's nuclear weapons facilities. Where we design and test nuclear weapons.
A journalist named Danny Casolaro spent a year pulling at the threads connecting PROMIS to Iran-Contra, B.C.C.I., intelligence agencies, and international weapons networks. He called it: The Octopus.
Iran-Contra. B.C.C.I. Intelligence agencies. International weapons networks. Same people. Every time.
In nineteen ninety-one his NSA source was beaten to death and found in the back seat of his car at Reagan National Airport.
On August tenth, nineteen ninety-one, Danny was found in a hotel bathtub in West Virginia. His wrists had been slashed at least ten times. Every page of his research was gone.
And his body was embalmed before his family was even told he was dead — which is illegal in West Virginia.
Later that same year Robert Maxwell fell off his yacht.
Soon after, his youngest daughter moved to New York and linked up with Jeffrey Epstein.
Three months later William Barr became Attorney General and put his own appointee in charge of the PROMIS investigation. The House Judiciary Committee called it a cover-up.
Barr also shut down the B.C.C.I. investigation and recommended the Iran-Contra pardons.
The New York Times started calling him: Cover-Up General.
Twenty-eight years later he was Attorney General again.
And on August tenth, twenty-eight years after Casolaro died, Jeffrey Epstein was found dead in a federal jail cell.
Guards falsified their logs or fell asleep.
He was taken off suicide watch.
Allowed to hoard linens. His roommate moved.
Cameras weren't working. Footage was destroyed.
He signed a new will two days earlier.
Which I'm sure is a completely normal sequence of coincidences that can definitely happen to the highest-profile inmate in the facility.
So just to recap. A husband and wife built revolutionary software, and were driven out of business by the government that took it. The back door was installed at a CIA-adjacent weapons facility on sovereign land where three people had already been killed for asking questions.
The journalist who connected the story died with his files missing. The man who had distributed the software died.
And twenty-eight years later, under the same Attorney General, Jeffrey Epstein allegedly died in his jail cell.
The FBI had a file on Robert Maxwell going back to the nineteen fifties. They watched him for decades. Yet he still managed to walk back-doored software into America's nuclear weapons facilities and later became one of the country's largest textbook publishers.
After he died, one daughter ran a sexual blackmail operation with Epstein, another became a bridge between Silicon Valley and Israeli intelligence, and another built the FBI's search system with the CIA's Chief Information Officer.
So I'm just curious whether the intelligence agencies are supposed to connect the dots —

or make sure no one ever does.
